Tedderfield's Top "Hazards" - Pilots Beware!

Peach Phantom Pastures 🍑

The ghostly remains of the peach orchards create phantom scents. If only!

Marauding Mowers 🚜

Local tractors occasionally decide to take a scenic spin, so a random mower on the edge of the Threshold or Runway isn't unheard of!

Cattle Conga Line 🐄

These cattle to the North of the airfield know how to line up across the fields, giving “moo-ve over” a whole new meaning.

Invasion of the Wattle Warriors 🌳🌿

These invasive trees are constantly sneaking closer to the airfield. In fact there is a whole contingent of them on the airfield itself. Watch out for an ambush of green along the edge of the runway.

Windy Whispers from the South 🌬️

Known to sweep through from the south on weekends, this breeze loves to test your approach skills to 29.

Klip Rivier's Funky Fresh Fragrance 🌊💨

Just north, the once clear and crisp Klip Rivier, was home to otters, now it offers up a bold fragrance - perfect for keeping you alert on the descent! No really, you have to be very low to participate in what the “Klip” has to offer!

The Stinky Four Dams of the Northwest 🐟

Known for a potent aroma, these dams provide the kind of ambiance that makes you appreciate a tightly sealed cockpit. Sorry microlights and open gyros!

Helicopter Hoverland 🚁

Choppers to the east of Tedderfield practice swooping and hovering, always ready to surprise you with a bit of impromptu airspace sharing. Keep a look out!

The Hidden Antenna Anomaly 📡

Barely visible until you're almost on top of it, this Vodacom antenna is here to keep you on your toes. There is a beacon at night, so keep a look out.

Notorious Dust Devil Zone 🌪️

Spotted in the open fields, these mini-twisters will give you an unrequested dusting - keep those wingtips steady!

The Powerline Gauntlet ⚡

Powerlines thread through the area like sneaky tripwires. Stay alert - you don't want to brush up against this particular hazard especially if you are dragging in on finals for 29.

Barbecue Breeze Blast 🍖💨

Saturday's FLY IN braais fill the air with irresistible scents of grilled boerewors and sometimes bacon and egg rolls, making every descent an exercise in focus.
